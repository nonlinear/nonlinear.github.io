Sterling introduces the concept of "spimes," which are objects that can be tracked through space and time via digital technology. The book explores the evolution from simple artifacts to complex, networked products, discussing how design, sustainability, and technology intersect. It is considered influential in design and speculative technology circles. {{< /recap >}} {{< recap title="A Terra dos Mil Povos" link="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1618852.A_terra_dos_mil_povos" image="https://example.com/a-terra-dos-mil-povos.jpg" >}} *A Terra dos Mil Povos* (1998) by Kaká Werá Jecupé is a non-fiction book that presents the history and culture of Brazil from the perspective of its indigenous peoples. The book combines historical accounts, cultural analysis, and personal narratives. It aims to highlight the diversity and contributions of native Brazilian societies, offering an alternative to mainstream historical narratives. The work is used as a reference in studies of indigenous history and rights in Brazil. {{< /recap >}} {{< recap title="Marx in the Anthropocene" link="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/60784441-marx-in-the-anthropocene" image="https://example.com/marx-in-the-athropocene.jpg" >}} *Marx in the Anthropocene* (2022) by Kohei Saito examines Karl Marx’s writings on ecology and the environment. The book argues that Marx developed a theory of sustainable development and was critical of capitalism’s impact on nature. Saito draws on Marx’s later works to show his relevance to current debates on climate change and ecological crisis. The book is recognized for its contribution to eco-socialist thought and environmental philosophy. {{< /recap >}} {{< recap title="Chimeras: Inventory of synthetic cognition" image="https://example.com/chimeras.jpg" link="https://www.onassis.org/culture/publications/chimeras-inventory-of-synthetic-cognition">}} {{< /recap>}} **Chimeras: Inventory of Synthetic Cognition** is a collaborative work published by Onassis Foundation in 2023. The book explores the intersection of artificial intelligence, art, and philosophy, featuring essays and projects from various contributors. It discusses how synthetic cognition is shaping creative practices and human understanding, blending theory with real-world examples. The collection is multidisciplinary, touching on technology, culture, and ethics. I read it in 2025. A Wizard of Earthsea and The Tombs of Atuan

**Earthsea Series** by Ursula K. Le Guin began in 1968 with "A Wizard of Earthsea." The series is a cornerstone of fantasy literature, following the wizard Ged as he grows and learns about power and balance. The books are known for their poetic language, deep themes, and unique world-building. They explore identity, coming of age, and the consequences of magic. I read these in 2025. If you want classic fantasy with depth, Earthsea is essential. {{< /recap >}} {{< recap title="The Velvet Rage" link="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/39700.The_Velvet_Rage" image="https://example.com/the-velvet-rage.jpg" >}} It is a mix of memoir, psychology, and self-help by Alan Downs, first published in 2005, focusing on the experiences of gay men in modern society. The book explores the roots of shame, the search for validation, and the journey toward self-acceptance. It is widely cited in LGBTQ+ discussions and therapy. I read it in 2025. It’s insightful and honest, offering both personal stories and practical advice. {{< /recap >}}
